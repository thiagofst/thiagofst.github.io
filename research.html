Research Overview

Exoplanets in Old and Chemically Distinct Stellar Populations

One core focus of my research concerns exoplanets orbiting chemically and kinematically peculiar stellar populations, particularly thick-disc and halo stars. These systems provide unique laboratories for studying planet formation in the early Galaxy under low-metallicity and α-enhanced conditions. We investigate how planet occurrence rates, bulk compositions, and orbital architectures differ from those around younger stellar populations, and how host-star chemistry and Galactic environemnt influence the long-term dynamical evolution of planetary systems (Ferreira & Galarza, 2025; arXiv:2512.17072). Related works explores warm Neptunes in the so-called Sub-Jovian desert, including prospects for atmospheric characterisation around old, low-mass stars and constraints on hydrodynamic escape processes (Ferreira & Galarza, in preparation).
