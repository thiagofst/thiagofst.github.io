I am a third-year PhD candidate in Astrophysics at Yale University, where I study the oldest and most chemically pristine stars in the Universe. My research sits within near-field cosmology, i.e., using the fossil record of ancient stellar populations to trace the structure and formation history of the Milky Way. I focus in particular on Population III and metal-poor stars, analysing their oscillation patterns, chemical abundances, and white dwarf remnants and their physics, both in isolation and in binary systems. Beyond stellar populations, I also investigate the evolution of exoplanets and substellar companions in wide binaries and dynamically complex environments. Before coming to Yale in 2023, I completed an MSc in Astronomy at the Universidade de São Paulo (IAG/USP-Brazil) and a BSc in Physics at the Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina (UFSC-Brazil).
