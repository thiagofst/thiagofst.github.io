About Me

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Astrophysics at Yale University, having moved to the U.S. in 2023. I hold an MSc in Astronomy from the University of SÃ£o Paulo (IAG/USP) and a BSc in Physics from the Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina (UFSC). My research focuses on stellar astrophysics across cosmic time, with particular emphasis on the structure, evolution, and observable signatures of low-metallicity and chemically pristine stars. I am especially interested in Population III and metal-poor stellar populations, stellar oscillations, abundances, and white dwarf remnants, both in isolation and in binary systems. More broadly, my work interfaces with Galactic archaeology — using stellar populations as tracers of Galactic structure and formation — and with the formation and evolution of exoplanets and substellar companions, particularly in wide binaries and dynamically complex environments.
